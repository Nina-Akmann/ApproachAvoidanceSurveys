The State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI)

Construct: Adult anxiety

Description of measurement: The State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI) is a commonly used measure of trait and state anxiety (Spielberger, Gorsuch, Lushene, Vagg, & Jacobs, 1983). It can be used in clinical settings to diagnose anxiety and to distinguish it from depressive syndromes.Anxiety Form Y, its most popular version, has 20 items for assessing trait anxiety and 20 for state anxiety. All items are rated on a 4-point scale (e.g., from “almost never” to “almost always”).

Psychometric properties: Internal consistency coefficients for the scale have ranged from .86 to .95; test-retest reliability coefficients have ranged from .65 to .75 over a 2-month interval (Spielberger et al., 1983). Test-retest coefficients for this measure in the present study ranged from .69 to .89. Considerable evidence attests to the construct and concurrent validity of the scale (Spielberger, 1989).

https://www.apa.org/pi/about/publications/caregivers/practice-settings/assessment/tools/trait-state

Spielberger, C. D. (1983). State-Trait Anxiety Inventory for Adults (STAI-AD). APA PsychTests. https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/t06496-000

(“The State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (Stai),” n.d.)

Intolerance of Uncertainty Scale (IUS)

Construct: Current research provides the result that intolerance of uncertainty may be a key construct in the emergence and maintenance of worrying. Worrying plays a role in both clinical and non-clinical studies. Researchers have been able to find a correlation between intolerance of uncertainty and worry. They suggest that people who are more likely to worry also have difficulty coping with uncertainty.This manifests itself in the fact that these individuals find it difficult to accept that a negative event could occur, even though the probability of its occurrence is perceived as low. Individuals with a tendency toward intolerance of uncertainty are less able to cope with the uncertainty and ambiguity of life.

Description of measurement: The original version of the IUS was developed in French. It measures emotional, cognitive, and behavioral responses to ambiguous situations, effects of uncertainty, and attempts to control the future. The IUS includes 27 items that relate to the idea that uncertainty is unacceptable, reflects poorly on a person, and leads to frustration, stress, and inability to act. Participants rate the items on a five-point Likert scale ranging from 1=‘not at all characteristic of me’ to 5=‘entirely characteristic of me’.

Psychometric properties: Both the English-translated and French versions of the IUS show excellent internal consistency (alpha=0.94/alpha=0.91) and good test-retest reliability (r=0.74/r=0.78) over a 5-week period. In addition, they show convergent and divergent validity with respect to measurement methods for worry, depression, and anxiety.

Buhr, K., & Dugas, M. J. (2002). The intolerance of uncertainty scale: psychometric properties of the English version. Behaviour Research and Therapy, 40, 931 - 945

BIS/ BAS scales

Construct: According to Gray (1981, 1982) individual behavior can be described by two distinct motivational systems. The BIS (behavioral inhibition system) regulates avoidance behavior when aversive stimuli are presented. The BAS (behavioral activation system) controls approach behavior in response to the presentation of appetitive stimuli. Through investigations in animals and pharmacological studies, sensitivity in these systems was found to be related to the personality dimensions of anxiety and impulsivity (Gray, 1991, 1994).

Description of measurement: Carver and White (1994) developed the BIS/ BAS scales for measuring individual dispositional differences in these two systems. The questionnaire consists of 24 items that represent a series of statements that can be used to describe oneself. Participants rate the extent to which the statements apply to them on a scale ranging from 1=‘does not apply to me at all’ to 4= ‘applies to me exactly’. In the present study we used the German version of the BIS/ BAS scale (Beauducel et al., 1999).

Psychometric properties: The German version of the BIS/ BAS scales shows a Cronbachs alpha for the BIS scale of alpha=0.78 and for the BAS alpha= 0.81. The discriminatory power of the items ranges from 0.28 to 0.64. The split-half reliability is r=0.79 for BIS and r=0.82 for BAS. The intercorrelations of the BIS/ BAS scales are moderate.

Carver, C. S., & White, T. L. (1994). Behavioral inhibition, behavioral activation, and affective responses to impending reward and punishment: The BIS/ BAS Scales. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 67(2), 319 – 333. https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/0022-3514.67.2.319

Strobel, A., Beauducel, A., Debener, S. & Brocke, B. (2001). Eine deutschsprachige Version des BIS/BAS Fragebogens von Carver und White. Zeitschrift für Differentielle und Diagnostische Psychologie, 22(3), 216 – 227. https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1024/0170-1789.22.3.216

(“Department of Psychology - BIS/BAS Scales,” n.d.)
